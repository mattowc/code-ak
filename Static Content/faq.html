How soon will I see benefits from the AcuKnee System?
Most people see significant immediate improvement within one hour of the first treatment.  Thereafter, the effects are cumulative and you will continue to improve for the first week.  A large percentage (depending on the severity of your condition) are pain free in less than a week.  The pain relief typically lasts about 24 hours.  We urge you to continue to treat for at least an additional week after the durable resolution of your pain.  Clients with osteoarthritis are stable in about a week but will need to treat indefinetly. There is no cure for osteoarthritis,  but the AcuKnee will eliminate or greatly reduce the need for pain medications and may slow the progression of the disease.
Why does this work for osteoarthritis?
Osteoarthritis, at its earliest stage and before you have even minimal pain, is characterized by inflammation.  This inflammation is chronic and ongoing.  The AcuKnee system, in addition to minimizing pain, reduces inflammation in the joint.  By reducing inflammation, you reduce pain and may reduce the risk of continung joint damage.

The AcuKnee is a meridian based precision electrotherapy system.  Accordingly, the direction of current flow is very important.  You will benefit from the treatment without changing the polarity but  we have found greater efficacy with strict attention to polarity.
I suffered a sports related overuse injury. Do I need to stop exercising while treating with AcuKnee?
Probably not.  Some prefer to rest and treat with the AcuKnee for several days.  Most people can resume LIGHT exercise in a gradual fashion as your symptoms improve.  DO NOT ICE the knee at any time if you are using the AcuKnee system.
I have a torn meniscus.  Can I treat with with the AcuKnee?
No. We do not recommend it.  The ongoing irritation of the meniscus or meniscal fragment within the joint greatly dimishes the effect and there will be no lasting resolution. In addition, there is a risk of the treatment itself further irritating the knee.
Why is it important to NOT ice while using the AcuKnee?
Ice is a great anti-inflammatory for acute injury. However, it has became clear to us that icing (especially before or after a teatment) diminishes or abolishes the positive effects of the AcuKnee treatment.

How often do I need to change the electrodes?
With daily use and meticulous care you should change them once a month. Careful attention to replacing the plastic electrode covers in the correct orientation--this side up-- as written on the plastic will further extend the electrode life.  In addition to a small amount of gel you should periodically  brush the electrodes gently with a used tooth brush dipped in warm water.  This allows the removal of accumulatd gel and dead skin cells that stick to th electrodes.  With older electrodes you may notice it takes larger amounts of current to feel the same stimulation effect.  As electrodes age,  the resistance increases.  Above all, when you remove the wrap or an electrode, pull very GENTLY as your peel off the wrap. This prevents delamination of the electrode.  If you fail to replace the electrode covers  ( with the correct side orientation ) after use they will dry out quickly and may not work well. When in doubt, change them.
